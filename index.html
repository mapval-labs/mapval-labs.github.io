Disambiguate your hardest problems

Understanding the ambiguous behavior of people is one of the most challenging objectives in business. Yet, as individuals and organizations, we often don't know how to effectively leverage the growing number of tools available to us for understanding human behavior. To achieve this objective in a world that is becoming increasingly complex and overlaid with data, we need to build a new layer of business education.

At Mapval, we’re building an MBA-like program focused on human behavior based on courseware that we’ve been teaching at Johns Hopkins University for the last two and a half years. There, we initially set out to help students understand algorithmic behavior but quickly realized that human behavior was driving all of the problems we examined.

With Mapval, we’re building a better way to make sense of human behavior in today’s complex operating environments. Our curriculum aims to connect the structure needed to understand algorithms with the empathy needed to understand humans.

Mapval is being developed by Leo Klenner, working with a network of partners across business and education. We’re currently in an early private beta and will share more details about our mission soon. You can connect with us at: hello@mapval.com.
